# The Relationship-Builder

The primary (and, for now, only) persona is a **general user — typically a
manager or someone who meets a lot of people**. They come back from meetings,
conferences, and hallway conversations with a head full of context that decays
fast: names, what was discussed, who else was in the room, and when it happened.

They don't want a CRM and they don't want forms. They want to **braindump** —
talk or type freely — and trust that the app keeps the notes organized so they
can recall and build on them later.

## Background

- Meets many people in short bursts (a conference day, a week of meetings).
- Thinks in terms of people *and* events ("that London week", "the three folks
  from the platform team").
- Limited time and patience for manual data entry.

## Goals

- Capture **who** they met, **what** they talked about, **when** it happened,
  and **whether more people** were in the meeting.
- Recall a person before the next conversation — what do I already know?
- Build **rapport** over time by remembering details, challenges, and promises.

## Frustrations

- Forgetting names and context days after a meeting.
- Notes scattered across apps, never revisited.
- Traditional tools make you stop and fill in fields instead of just talking.

## How the product helps

- **Chat is the only input.** They braindump in natural language; an LLM agent
  does the organizing.
- The agent **asks follow-up questions** (what challenge were they solving? what
  did you learn?) so the captured context is rich enough to build rapport.
- Recall happens through read-only views — the Dictionary surface,
  Timeline surface, and Follow ups surface.
