# Timeline

The timeline is the **chronological, cross-person** surface. Where the
Dictionary surface is organized by *person*, the timeline is organized by
*time* and treats the **event** as the primary unit — a meeting can involve
several people and topics at once.

## Purpose

- Answer *"What was that series of meetings last week?"* or *"What happened at
  DevConf?"* — context that a per-person view scatters.
- Give a single scannable history after a busy stretch like a conference.

## Key elements

- A reverse-chronological list of events.
- Each entry shows the date, the person, the context (conference / office /
  call), co-attendees, and notes.
- Person names deep-link into the Dictionary surface.

## Why it is not redundant with the Dictionary

The dictionary is *entity lookup* ("remind me who Jane is"). The timeline is
*contextual recall* ("what was that week of meetings about"). The event — which
groups multiple people and topics — only exists as a first-class thing here.

All entries originate from the Chat surface.
